Information

Harley-Davidson's status as a global icon is rooted in its remarkable survival and unique engineering, having emerged from a 10x15-foot backyard shed in 1903 to become one of only two major American manufacturers to survive the Great Depression. The brand is famously defined by its "potato-potato" engine sound, an asymmetrical rumble created by a single-pin crankshaft that the company unsuccessfully tried to trademark in the 1990s. Beyond the mechanics, the bikes carry the nickname "Hogs," a term coined in the 1920s by a racing team that carried a live piglet as their mascot on victory laps. This legacy of rebellion and freedom was further cemented by Harley-Davidson's extensive wartime service—supplying over 90,000 "Liberator" motorcycles to the military during WWII—and its starring roles in counterculture films like The Wild One and Easy Rider.

List of most popular models currently

As of 2026, Harley-Davidson’s most popular models are led by the Street Glide, which remains the brand's best-selling touring motorcycle due to its balance of long-distance comfort and everyday rideability.

Grand American Touring (Top Sellers) Street Glide®: The most iconic "bagger," featuring the signature batwing fairing. Road Glide®: Popular for its frame-mounted "sharknose" fairing, which provides superior stability at highway speeds. Street Glide/Road Glide Limited: New for 2026, these "full-dress" models include heated seats, grips, and a larger "Grand Tour-Pak" for maximum storage. Cruisers (Performance & Style) Low Rider® S / ST: A favorite for "club-style" performance riders, featuring the high-output Milwaukee-Eight 117 engine. Fat Boy®: Famous for its massive 240mm rear tire and solid-disc "Lakester" wheels; it remains one of Harley's most recognizable icons. Street Bob®: Often cited as the best entry-point for the cruiser lineup due to its lighter weight and bobber-style handlebars. Heritage Classic: Blends 1950s nostalgia with modern tech; it features the lowest seat height in the cruiser category (26 inches).
